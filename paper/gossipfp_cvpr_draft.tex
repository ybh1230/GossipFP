\begin{abstract}
Semi-supervised semantic segmentation depends on pseudo labels, but pseudo labels are often biased near semantic boundaries and confusing classes. Density-descending feature perturbation explores low-density regions, yet it requires an auxiliary online density estimator and the resulting direction is not explicitly tied to the classes that the teacher currently confuses. We propose Gossip-Guided Feature Perturbation (GossipFP), a class-level information exchange mechanism for semantic boundary exploration. GossipFP maintains one prototype per class, builds a normalized relation graph from prototype similarity and teacher confusion, and performs a few lazy gossip diffusion rounds. The key object is not the diffused consensus itself, but the residual between a class prototype and its gossip consensus. This residual gives a compact semantic boundary direction for adversarial feature perturbation. The final method intentionally uses a fixed perturbation radius and removes reliability scores, adaptive radii, and mandatory auxiliary separation losses, making the contribution easier to analyze and ablate.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Semi-supervised semantic segmentation aims to learn dense visual representations from a small labeled set and a large unlabeled set. A common solution is teacher-student consistency: an exponential moving average teacher predicts pseudo labels, and the student is trained to keep those labels under strong augmentation \cite{tarvainen2017mean}. This works well on confident regions, but the teacher can repeatedly underexplore pixels near semantic boundaries, rare classes, and visually similar categories.

Feature-level perturbation is a direct way to probe these weak regions. DDFP perturbs features toward lower-density regions estimated by a normalizing flow \cite{wang2024ddfp}. The density view is meaningful under the low-density separation assumption, but it has two limitations for dense prediction. First, the normalizing flow is a second trainable model optimized online on high-dimensional features. Second, a low-density direction is class-agnostic: it may move a road feature into a sparse feature region without specifically testing the road-sidewalk, road-car, or road-building boundary.

We replace density estimation with class-level gossip. The term ``gossip'' is used carefully. Graph regularization and graph neural networks propagate features or labels so neighboring nodes become smoother \cite{kipf2017gcn}. Contrastive learning selects positives and negatives so representations are pulled together or pushed apart \cite{he2020moco,li2021pcl}. GossipFP has a different target. It lets semantic class nodes exchange prototype information, but it does not use the consensus as a smoothed prediction and does not use neighbors merely as contrastive negatives. Instead, it uses the residual between the original class prototype and the local gossip consensus as the perturbation direction. Thus graph regularization asks for agreement, contrastive learning asks for separation, and GossipFP asks: \emph{where does a class disagree with its confusing semantic neighborhood, and can the student remain stable there?}

This residual view is the reason multi-round gossip is useful beyond a single weighted prototype average. A one-hop average describes only immediate nearest classes. Lazy diffusion repeatedly mixes local neighborhoods, so the residual captures a higher-order class boundary field induced by the current relation graph. We use only a few rounds to avoid oversmoothing. The method remains small: no learned graph module, no class-dependent reliability formula, no adaptive perturbation radius, and no mandatory margin loss.

Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item We reformulate feature perturbation for semi-supervised segmentation as class-level gossip residual exploration, replacing an online density estimator with a lightweight semantic memory.
    \item We build a normalized prototype-confusion graph that uses both semantic proximity and the teacher's current confusion, while avoiding unnormalized score addition.
    \item We introduce residual-guided perturbation and include a direct ``residual vs. consensus state'' ablation to show that the residual, not graph smoothing alone, is the useful signal.
    \item We provide runnable PASCAL VOC and Cityscapes training code, ablation configs, and a residual visualization tool for class-wise boundary analysis.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Semi-supervised semantic segmentation.}
Teacher-student training is widely used for semi-supervised dense prediction. The teacher provides pseudo labels, while the student learns from augmented inputs. The main risk is confirmation bias: wrong or incomplete pseudo labels can become self-reinforcing, especially around ambiguous boundaries.

\paragraph{Feature perturbation.}
Adversarial and feature-level perturbations regularize a classifier by probing local neighborhoods of the representation. DDFP perturbs features toward low-density regions estimated by a normalizing flow \cite{wang2024ddfp,dinh2017realnvp}. GossipFP keeps the same broad goal of boundary exploration, but makes the perturbation class-conditional through prototype information exchange.

\paragraph{Gossip strategy.}
Gossip protocols exchange information through local communication without a central coordinator \cite{kempe2003gossip}. Optimization methods often apply gossip to parameters. For semantic segmentation, we argue that class prototypes are the right granularity: they are cheap to store, interpretable, and directly aligned with dense semantic errors.

\section{Method}

\subsection{Training Framework}

Let $\mathcal{D}_l=\{(x_i,y_i)\}$ be labeled images and $\mathcal{D}_u=\{u_i\}$ be unlabeled images. A student network $S$ is trained with a teacher $T$ updated by exponential moving average. The teacher predicts pseudo labels $\hat{Y}$ on weakly augmented unlabeled images, and pixels below confidence threshold $\gamma$ are ignored. GossipFP modifies the perturbation branch: instead of using a density gradient, it perturbs decoder features along class-level gossip residuals.

\subsection{Prototype and Confusion Memory}

GossipFP maintains a normalized prototype $p_c \in \mathbb{R}^d$ for each class $c$. Given teacher features $f_i$ and labels $\tilde{y}_i$ from ground-truth or confident pseudo-labeled pixels, the batch prototype is
\begin{equation}
    \bar{p}_c =
    \mathrm{norm}
    \left(
    \frac{1}{|\Omega_c|}
    \sum_{i \in \Omega_c}
    \mathrm{norm}(f_i)
    \right),
    \quad
    \Omega_c=\{i \mid \tilde{y}_i=c\}.
\end{equation}
If class $c$ has enough pixels in the distributed mini-batch, its memory is updated by
\begin{equation}
    p_c \leftarrow \mathrm{norm}\left(m p_c + (1-m)\bar{p}_c\right).
\end{equation}
A class observed for the first time is initialized directly by $\bar{p}_c$.

The teacher also provides a confusion statistic. For pixels assigned to class $c$, we average the teacher probability of class $j$:
\begin{equation}
    q_{c,j} =
    \frac{\sum_{i \in \Omega_c} a_i P_T(j\mid x_i)}
    {\sum_{i \in \Omega_c} a_i},
\end{equation}
where $a_i=1$ for ground-truth labels and $a_i$ is the teacher confidence for pseudo labels. Low-confidence pseudo labels are already ignored by the training threshold. The diagonal of $q$ is set to zero and the matrix is updated with momentum. A class joins the graph once it has a prototype and enough accumulated pixels. This simple readiness rule replaces the previous reliability-score design.

\subsection{Normalized Gossip Relation Graph}

For class $c$, valid neighbors are observed non-self classes. Directly adding prototype similarity and teacher confusion is poorly conditioned because the two rows can have different scales. We therefore standardize each relation row over valid neighbors:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{Z}(M)_{c,j}
    =
    \frac{M_{c,j}-\mu_c(M)}
    {\sqrt{\sigma_c^2(M)+\epsilon_z}}.
\end{equation}
The edge score is
\begin{equation}
    s_{c,j}
    =
    (1-\lambda)\mathcal{Z}(\cos(p_c,p_j))_{c,j}
    +
    \lambda \mathcal{Z}(q)_{c,j},
    \quad j \ne c,
\end{equation}
where $\lambda$ mixes semantic proximity and teacher confusion. Each node keeps its top-$K$ outgoing neighbors and normalizes them with a softmax temperature $\tau$:
\begin{equation}
    A_{c,j} =
    \frac{\exp(s_{c,j}/\tau)}
    {\sum_{k \in \mathcal{N}_c}\exp(s_{c,k}/\tau)},
    \quad j\in\mathcal{N}_c.
\end{equation}
This graph is not a learned GNN layer. It is a compact class communication map that asks which semantic classes should exchange information before generating perturbations.

\subsection{Lazy Gossip Residual}

Initialize $h_c^{(0)}=p_c$. GossipFP runs a small number of lazy diffusion rounds:
\begin{equation}
    h_c^{(r+1)}
    =
    \mathrm{norm}
    \left(
    \frac{1}{2}h_c^{(r)}
    +
    \frac{1}{2}\sum_j A_{c,j}h_j^{(r)}
    \right),
    \quad r=0,\ldots,R-1.
\end{equation}
The fixed half self-loop removes an extra hyperparameter and prevents a class from being overwritten by its neighbors. Ignoring the final normalization for analysis, the update is $H^{(r+1)}=PH^{(r)}$ with $P=\frac{1}{2}I+\frac{1}{2}A$. Since $A$ is row-stochastic, $P$ is also row-stochastic and lazy. Repeated application moves connected components toward consensus, but GossipFP deliberately stops after a small $R$.

The perturbation field is the residual
\begin{equation}
    b_c = h_c^{(R)} - p_c.
\end{equation}
This residual is a graph high-pass signal: it is small when a prototype agrees with its local semantic neighborhood and large when the neighborhood pulls it toward a different state. Using $b_c$ rather than $h_c^{(R)}$ is central. The consensus state is a smoothed class representation; the residual is the boundary-seeking direction.

\subsection{Residual-Guided Perturbation}

For a decoder feature $z_i$ with pseudo label $\hat{y}_i$, GossipFP maximizes alignment with the normalized class residual:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L}_{adv}
    =
    \frac{1}{|\Omega|}
    \sum_{i\in\Omega}
    \left\langle
    \mathrm{norm}(z_i),
    \mathrm{norm}(b_{\hat{y}_i})
    \right\rangle.
\end{equation}
The perturbation is a single fixed-radius normalized gradient step:
\begin{equation}
    r =
    \epsilon
    \frac{\nabla_r \mathcal{L}_{adv}(z(F+r),\hat{Y})}
    {\|\nabla_r \mathcal{L}_{adv}(z(F+r),\hat{Y})\|_2+\epsilon_g}.
\end{equation}
The gradient is the tangent-space projection induced by feature normalization, not an algebraic claim that the gradient is exactly $h_c-p_c$. This makes the direction statement technically precise. The residual branch is compared with a consensus-state ablation that replaces $\mathrm{norm}(b_c)$ by $\mathrm{norm}(h_c^{(R)})$ under the same graph and training schedule.

The perturbed features are passed through the classifier and trained with the original pseudo labels. We use a fixed $\epsilon$ rather than an adaptive class radius. This keeps the method focused: the experiment tests the value of the gossip residual itself, not the interaction of residuals with extra uncertainty heuristics.

\subsection{Overall Objective}

The training objective is
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{L}
    =
    \mathcal{L}_{sup}
    +
    \lambda_u \mathcal{L}_{u}
    +
    \lambda_p \mathcal{L}_{u}^{gossip},
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{L}_{sup}$ is the supervised segmentation loss, $\mathcal{L}_{u}$ is the standard pseudo-label loss on strongly augmented unlabeled images, and $\mathcal{L}_{u}^{gossip}$ is the pseudo-label loss on gossip-perturbed features.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{GossipFP Training}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE Initialize student $S$, teacher $T$, and class prototype memory.
\FOR{each mini-batch}
    \STATE Predict pseudo labels with teacher $T$ and ignore low-confidence pixels.
    \STATE Update class prototypes and teacher confusion statistics.
    \STATE Build a row-normalized prototype-confusion graph.
    \STATE Run $R$ lazy gossip rounds and compute residuals $b_c=h_c-p_c$.
    \STATE Generate fixed-radius residual-guided feature perturbations.
    \STATE Train $S$ with supervised, pseudo-label, and gossip-perturbed pseudo-label losses.
    \STATE Update $T$ with EMA of $S$.
\ENDFOR
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Efficiency}

GossipFP removes the normalizing-flow density estimator and its optimizer. The remaining overhead consists of class-wise feature accumulation, relation construction, and sparse gossip diffusion. Dense relation construction costs $O(C^2d)$ for $C$ classes and prototype dimension $d$, while top-$K$ diffusion costs $O(RCKd)$. Since $C=21$ for PASCAL VOC and $C=19$ for Cityscapes, this overhead is negligible compared with the backbone forward pass. For datasets with hundreds of classes, the same formulation can update the graph less frequently or use approximate top-$K$ retrieval.

\section{Conclusion}

This paper proposed GossipFP, a simplified class-level gossip residual method for semi-supervised semantic segmentation. By replacing density descent with prototype-confusion information exchange, GossipFP produces semantic boundary perturbations without an auxiliary density estimator. The final design centers on one claim: multi-round class gossip is useful because its residual exposes confusing semantic boundaries, and this residual is a better perturbation target than the smoothed consensus state itself.
